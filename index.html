Rock Cycle

The rock cycle is how rocks change from one type to another

There are three types of rocks:

Igneous Rock

  • How it forms: Igneous rocks form when magma (molten rock) cools and hardens.

    • Type:
  • Intrusive (forms inside the Earth, cools slowly, large crystals) – like granite.
  • Extrusive (forms on the surface, cools quickly, small or no crystals) – like basalt.
  • Example: Obsidian, pumice, granite.

Sedimentary Rock

  • How it forms: Sedimentary rocks form from layers of sand, mud, or other small pieces of rock that are pressed together over time.
  • Often formed in, river, lakes, or oceans.
  • Can contain fossils.
  • Example: Sandstone, limestone, shale.

Metamorphic Rock

  • How it forms: rocks form when existing rocks (igneous or sedimentary) are changed by heat and pressure deep inside the Earth.
  • The rock doesn't melt, but its structure and minerals change.
  • Example: Marble (from limestone), slate (from shale).

Then the cycle repeats.
